How to use the visualization and general curling knowledge:

  • Hovering over any bold or colored text in this section will highlight the subject of the text.
  • Rocks can be moved by clicking and dragging.
  • A rock enters play when it completely crosses the hogline or comes into contact with another rock.
  • A rock is considered to be in play until it completely crosses the backline.
  • If any part of a rock is touching the house then it is considered to be inside the house.
  • Rocks inside the house are called draws.
  • If only part of a rock is inside the house it is called a biter.
  • Rocks outside of the house and in front of the tee line are called guards.
  • When a rock is dragged inside the house, a circle will appear to help compare the distance from each rock to the pin.
